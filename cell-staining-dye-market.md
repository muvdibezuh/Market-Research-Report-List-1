<p><p>The cell staining dye market is categorized into three main types: acidic, basic, and neutral cell stains. Acidic cell stains, such as eosin, are used to highlight structures with a positive charge, often targeting proteins. Basic cell stains, like hematoxylin, bind to negatively charged components, commonly staining nucleic acids. Neutral cell stains, such as neutral red, provide a balanced approach, suitable for various cellular components. Each type serves distinct purposes in cell biology, histology, and medical diagnostics.</p></p>
