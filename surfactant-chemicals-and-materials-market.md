<p><p>Surfactant chemicals are crucial in various industries, categorized into four main types: anionic, cationic, amphoteric, and nonionic. Anionic surfactants carry a negative charge, making them effective for cleaning and emulsifying. Cationic surfactants, with a positive charge, are used in disinfectants and hair conditioners. Amphoteric surfactants can function as either anionic or cationic based on pH, offering versatility in formulations. Nonionic surfactants, lacking a charge, provide mildness and stability, making them suitable for delicate applications. Each type serves unique functions in cleaning, personal care, and industrial processes.</p></p>
