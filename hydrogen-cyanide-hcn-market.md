<p><p>Hydrogen Cyanide (HCN) is primarily produced through various processes, including the Andrussow and Acrylonitrile methods. The Andrussow process synthesizes HCN via the reaction of methane, ammonia, and air at high temperatures. The Acrylonitrile process produces HCN as a byproduct in the manufacture of acrylonitrile from propylene. Other methods may include the formamide and sodium cyanide processes. Each method varies in efficiency, cost, and environmental impact, influencing market dynamics and production capacities across different regions.</p></p>
